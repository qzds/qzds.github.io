5. Sample stability assessment

To ensure the stability of DNA encapsulation products in harsh environments, we simulate five different environments: high oxidation, high salt, ultraviolet light, freezing, and aging based on laboratory conditions. Experiments show that the encapsulated samples are more stable than the unencapsulated samples, which makes up for the shortcomings of bare DNA and expands the application scenarios of DNA anti-counterfeiting traceability technology.

（1）Antioxidant
Experimental materials:
  • DNA anti-counterfeiting product
  • hydrogen peroxide
  • NaOH solution
  • HCl solution
Experimental procedure：
  1. The encapsulated DNA group and the unencapsulated DNA group were treated with different concentrations of hydrogen peroxide: 0%, 0.5%, 1%, 2% H₂O₂, and ddH₂O was added to the control group.
  2. After treatment, NaOH base was used to crack, then neutralized with HCl, and finally diluted with ddH₂O and mixed well. Detected as a qPCR template.
（2）Anti-osmolarity
Experimental materials:
  • DNA anti-counterfeiting product
  • NaCl solution
  • NaOH solution
  • HCl solution
Experimental procedure：
  1. The encapsulated DNA group and the unencapsulated DNA group were treated with different concentrations of NaCl solution: 0M, 0.2M, 0.5M, and 1M NaCl, and ddH₂O was added to the control group.
  2. After treatment, NaOH base was used to crack, then neutralized with HCl, and finally diluted with ddH₂O and mixed well. Detected as a qPCR template.
（3）UV resistant
Experimental materials:
  • DNA anti-counterfeiting products
  • ultraviolet lamps
  • NaOH solution
  • HCl solution
Experimental procedure：
  1. Prepare two sets of cuvettes: group A (encapsulated DNA) and group B (unencapsulated DNA). Add 500 μL of the corresponding DNA solution to each group. Place the cuvette vertically under the UV lamp in the ultra-clean workbench, about 50 cm away from the UV lamp, turn on the UV lamp and start the timer.
  2. Take 10 μL of samples every 30 min, place them in PCR vials, label the processing time, and store the samples at 4 °C. Continue UV exposure until the cumulative treatment time reaches 3 hours.
（4）Freeze-thaw stability
Experimental materials:
  • DNA anti-counterfeiting product
  • -80 degree refrigerator
  • NaOH solution
  • HCl solution
Experimental procedure：
  1. Prepare two sets of samples: encapsulated DNA set and unencapsulated DNA set, put the samples in the -80 °C refrigerator, remove every 30 minutes and thaw in a 37 °C water bath for 3-5 minutes, perform qPCR testing after thawing, and return the rest of the samples to the freezer to continue freezing and thawing.
  2. Repeat the freeze-thaw operation until it is completed 4 times. The thawed samples were lysed with NaOH base, neutralized with HCl, and finally diluted with ddH₂O and mixed well for qPCR detection.
（5）Accelerated aging
Experimental materials:
  • DNA anti-counterfeiting products
  • magnesium chloride
  • sodium bromide
  • sodium chloride saturated salt solution
  • water bath
  • NaOH solution
  • HCl solution
Experimental procedure：
  1. Three saturated salt solutions (magnesium chloride, sodium bromide, and sodium chloride) were prepared to simulate air humidity of 25%, 50%, and 75%, respectively.
  2. Prepare tubes with encapsulated and unencapsulated DNA, and set up parallel controls for each set. Place the PCR tube into the EP tube, add the corresponding saturated salt solution and make sure there is a solid at the bottom to maintain stable humidity.
  3. Place the EP tube in a water bath pot at a set temperature (60°C, 65°C, 70°C). After adding NaOH to heat denaturation every 72 hours, add HCl to neutralize. Dilute with ddH₂O and mix the diluted sample and use it for qPCR detection.
  4. According to the Arrhenius formula, the function of the reaction half-life t1/2 about temperature is obtained.
  5. Draw an accelerated aging curve.
