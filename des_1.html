Technical Process

For DNA anti-counterfeiting, we have created a complete set of technical processes.

Based on our proprietary DNA encoding/decoding and nucleic acid rapid detection technologies, we utilize DNA as a carrier of anti-counterfeiting information. Customized information is encoded into readable and writable standard DNA fingerprint sequences, which are then synthesized into actual single-stranded short DNA sequences via chemical encoding. Through biological encoding, these are further transformed into long-chain DNA fingerprint plasmids containing traceability and anti-counterfeiting information. The DNA fingerprint samples are mass-produced rapidly and cost-effectively using PCR and biological methods, followed by nano-encapsulation and bio-encapsulation to form stable anti-counterfeiting nanomaterials capable of storing anti-counterfeiting information. These materials can be seamlessly embedded into or attached to products, and remain identifiable for centuries.

Workflow

Input Data

Receive product information coding and raw material label information

Message Transcoding

Convert product traceability information to standard DNA base sequence

Fingerprint Code

Sequence synthesis based on chemical/biological coding technology

Fingerprint Preparation

DNA fingerprinting based on PCR technology and artificial cell synthesis

Marker Package

Nano/bio-encapsulation and product labeling of DNA fingerprints

Message Decoding

Instant and accurate decoding of information based on nucleic acid snapshot/fingerprint sequencing technology

Based on the characteristics of high-density information storage and easy degradation of DNA molecules, the problems that need to be solved mainly include: designing reasonable algorithms to achieve efficient encoding of DNA base information, and constructing reasonable DNA macromolecular structures to achieve effective preservation of anti-counterfeiting information.
