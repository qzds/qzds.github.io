1. DNA Sequence Design and Encoding

Purpose

To computationally design and synthesize unique, product-specific DNA sequences that serve as the unforgeable molecular core of the XMU-HeliCipher tag, ensuring high information density and biochemical stability.

Materials
  • Standardized anti-counterfeiting information (containing numbers and letters, e.g., "mdscblcgx20220309")
Procedure
  1. Control Settings: Unified test set: Blawat algorithm (static base mapping) and DNA fountain algorithm (random base allocation), tested synchronously with our algorithm (dynamic matching + hierarchical compression).
  2. The three algorithms encoded data samples respectively to generate corresponding DNA sequences;
  3. Detection indicators were calculated, and decoding was performed to determine the accuracy rate.
Result
Comparative analysis of DNA encoding algorithms performance
Interpretation

Our algorithm outperforms others as shown in the figures.

  1. GC Stability: By real-time calling of the candidate base combination library, the dynamic matching algorithm locks GC content within 48%-52% (bandwidth 4%), far superior to Blawat (35%-48%, bandwidth 13%) and DNA fountain (38%-50%, bandwidth 12%). This supports PCR amplification efficiency exceeding 98% (Blawat: 85%; DNA fountain: 90%).
  2. Homopolymer Control: With real-time verification of preceding bases, the algorithm achieves an average homopolymer length of 1.2 bp; Blawat averages 1.6 bp (up to 1.8 bp in some cases); DNA fountain averages 1.4 bp.
  3. Encoding Efficiency: Through predefined short codes + differential encoding, the sequence length concentrates in 54-75 bp, 25% shorter than Blawat (72-100 bp) and 12%-15% shorter than DNA fountain (63-88 bp), compatible with plasmid insertion limits.
  4. Decoding Reliability: Relying on verification bases + redundant storage of metadata, the accuracy remains 100% for sample sizes ranging from 1000 to 100000.

The experiment confirms that the dynamic matching encoding algorithm comprehensively outperforms Blawat and DNA fountain in key indicators. Its dynamic adjustment and compression mechanisms not only meet theoretical performance requirements but also adapt to the practical needs of the entire experimental workflow, providing core algorithmic support for the XMU-HeliCipher anti-counterfeiting system.
