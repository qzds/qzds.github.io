Molecular Structure Design

To effectively encapsulate DNA molecules, we selected gelatin and chitosan as the encapsulation materials. Under acidic conditions, both gelatin and chitosan become positively charged and can adsorb negatively charged DNA through electrostatic interactions. Glutaraldehyde and vanillin were then used as crosslinking agents to react with the materials, forming a three-dimensional networked spherical structure that "locks" the DNA inside. To enhance long-term anti-counterfeiting durability, a silica shell was coated around the structure, resulting in the final DNA-based anti-counterfeiting nanomolecule—Helicipher.
