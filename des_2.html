Encoding Algorithm

We designed the dynamic relationship between base sequence and information unit, avoided the problem of single base duplication, effectively improved the storage density (from 4 bases per character to 3 bases per character), which substantially reduces homopolymer-induced synthesis errors, increases strand synthesis success rate, and lowers overall synthesis cost per encoded unit.

DNA Encoding Algorithm

The principle of DNA encoding algorithms can be understood as "translating letters and numbers into DNA base sequences." In this context, the four bases—A, T, C, and G—act like phonetic letters, and through different combinations, they can correspond to the 26 English letters and 10 digits. The detailed workflow is shown in the figure below.

After the original sequence is preprocessed into a meta-compressed sequence, it is dynamically mapped to 36 DNA base combinations (derived from 4×3×3, with "4" referring to the 4 DNA bases: A (Adenine), T (Thymine), C (Cytosine), G (Guanine)). The optimal combination set is selected per predefined criteria—to avoid single-base repetition (e.g., consecutive "AAAA") and secondary structures (e.g., hairpins/stem-loops that interfere with sequencing/synthesis) and brought positive effects to DNA synthesis.

Compression Principle

The compression principle is like "spotting repeated parts and replacing them with shorter symbols." The system first identifies repeated segments, then creates a "lookup table" to map them to shorter codes. This process is repeated several times, making the data take up less space while also making the DNA sequence more suitable for storage and use.

DNA Decoding Algorithm

During decoding, the DNA sequence is first obtained by sequencing. Then, the "lookup table information" added during compression is removed, and the sequence goes through error detection and preprocessing. After that, the sequence is gradually restored to text or data according to the original coding rules.

Finally, a verification mechanism checks the result to ensure the anti-counterfeiting and traceability information is accurately recovered.
