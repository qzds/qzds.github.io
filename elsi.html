Ethical Issues

The DNA used is fully synthetic, non-biological, and incapable of replication. Encapsulated in multiple protective layers, it poses no risk of biological leakage or environmental dissemination. Waste materials can be disposed of as ordinary trash, fully ensuring ecological and public safety.

Legal Issues

The technology does not qualify as Living Modified Organisms (LMOs) under the Cartagena Protocol. Moreover, DNA authentication results provide legally admissible evidence in court for resolving product authenticity disputes. The system does not collect personal or state-sensitive data, strictly complies with international and domestic data regulations such as GDPR and China's Data Security Law, and will incorporate third-party auditing mechanisms to enhance compliance and transparency.

Social Issues

Through clear labeling and science communication, the project proactively addresses potential public concerns about "DNA" tags, emphasizing that they are solely used for anti-counterfeiting verification and do not involve genetic modification or data privacy issues. This technology provides a powerful tool against counterfeit products, protects consumer rights and brand interests, and helps foster a secure and trustworthy market environment.

Conclusion

With innovation at its core, XMU-HeliCipher fulfills ethical, legal, and social responsibilities in a comprehensive manner, offering a safe, credible, and socially beneficial model for the large-scale commercial application of DNA-based anti-counterfeiting technology.
